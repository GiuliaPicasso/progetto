Codice Folklore is the visualization of the data collection created as a conclusion to the research focused on the translation and reinvention of Japanese folklore in animation, and how animation manages to convey cultural meanings to a new audience. The title is the Italian translation of folklore code

For this reason, Codice Folklore analyzes only a portion of the vastness of Japanese anime (Timeline), identifying and pinpointing the elements of nature and the supernatural to delve into the folkloric quality encoded in its visual representation. The data is divided into the categories of Kami, Yōkai, and Shizenrei. Index is a research tool that compiles and alphabetically indexes the outcomes of the data collection.
The websites used are listed below:

  • Japanese Animated Film Classics
  • Anilist.co
  • Yokai.com

    • The database purposely implies two different types of exploring through scrolling, miming Japanese traditional art and scrolls (emakimono):

    ↔ horizontal scrolling: discovering
    ↕ vertical scrolling: deepen the information

    The data I collected focus only on a portion of the whole symbolism behind the visual representation in Japanese traditional animation. Anyone who is interested can freely take on from this research and continue it under a different perspective, using different filters or analysing different mediums.
