Codice Folklore is the digital archive of the data collected during the research for my Master's thesis in Communication design, focused on the translation and reinvention of Japanese folklore in animation, and how animation manages to convey cultural meanings to a new audience. Hence the title, that is the Italian translation of "folklore code". For this reason, this online archive contains only a portion of the vastness of Japanese anime (Timeline), identifying and pinpointing the elements of nature and the supernatural to delve into the folkloric quality encoded in its visual representation. The data is divided into the categories of Kami, Y≈çkai, and Shizenrei. Index is a research tool that compiles and alphabetically indexes the outcomes of the data collection. Most of the content used for the research comes from the following online databases: Japanese Animated Film Classics , Anilist.co , Yokai.com.

The data I collected focus only on a portion of the whole symbolism behind the visual representation in Japanese traditional animation. Anyone who is interested can freely take on from this research and continue it under a different perspective, using different filters or analysing different mediums.
